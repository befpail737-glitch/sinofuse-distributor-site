PV Inverter DC Side Protection: A Complete Application Guide

By Jane Smith, FAEPublished on: August 10, 2025

Protecting the DC side of a photovoltaic (PV) system is a unique challenge that requires specialized components. Unlike traditional AC circuits, PV arrays are current-limited power sources where fault conditions can be subtle yet dangerous. This application note provides a comprehensive guide on using Sinofuse PV series fuses to ensure the safety and reliability of your solar inverter designs.

Understanding the "gPV" Characteristic

The most critical factor in PV protection is understanding the gPV fuse class, as defined by the IEC 60269-6 standard. A gPV fuse is a full-range breaking capacity fuse designed specifically for photovoltaic applications. Its key feature is the ability to reliably interrupt very low overload currents.

In a solar array, a fault (e.g., from a shaded or damaged panel) might only produce a current that is 1.3 to 1.5 times the nominal operating current (Isc). A standard gG fuse would likely fail to open under these conditions, allowing the fault to persist and potentially cause a fire. A gPV fuse is guaranteed to open safely at these low multiples of its rated current, isolating the faulty string.

Sizing the String Fuse: A Step-by-Step Calculation

The primary role of the string fuse is to protect against reverse currents. To size it correctly, follow these steps based on the solar panel's datasheet:

  1. Determine Maximum Series Fuse Rating: Find the "Maximum Series Fuse Rating" on the panel's datasheet. Your selected fuse must have a current rating (In) less than or equal to this value.
  2. Apply Sizing Factor: The fuse rating (In) should be greater than or equal to 1.56 times the panel's Short Circuit Current (Isc). This factor accounts for solar irradiance gain and ensures the fuse does not nuisance trip under peak sun conditions.

Example: A panel has an Isc of 10A and a Maximum Series Fuse Rating of 20A. The minimum fuse rating would be 10A * 1.56 = 15.6A. Therefore, a 20A gPV fuse from the PV312 Series would be the correct choice.

Voltage Rating and Breaking Capacity

The voltage rating of the fuse must be greater than or equal to the maximum open-circuit voltage (Voc) of the solar string, including temperature corrections. For modern systems, this means selecting a fuse rated for 1500VDC. The breaking capacity must be sufficient to handle the worst-case fault current from all parallel strings, which is why a rating of 30kA or higher is standard.
