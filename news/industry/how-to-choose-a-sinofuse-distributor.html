First Look: A Review of the Sinofuse BS Series Fuses

... Author Info ...

Sinofuse recently expanded its portfolio with the introduction of the new BS Series, targeting high-performance auxiliary circuit protection in demanding applications like electric vehicles and energy storage. As an authorized distributor, we got our hands on some of the first samples to see how they stack up. Here's our FAE team's review.

First Impressions: Compact and Robust

The first thing you notice about the BS Series is its compact, block-style design. Compared to older cylindrical or blade-style fuses for similar ratings, the BS series offers a significant space saving on the PCB or busbar. The housing is made from a high-temperature, UL 94-V0 rated polymer, giving it a very solid and robust feel suitable for harsh environments.

Key Features and Performance

We see three standout features that make this series compelling:

  1. High DC Voltage Rating: With a rating of 500VDC, this series is perfectly suited for protecting auxiliary circuits (like DC/DC converters, contactor coils, and onboard chargers) in modern 400V and even some 800V EV architectures.
  2. Low I²t Value: The fuse has a very low melting integral (I²t), making it fast-acting. This is crucial for protecting sensitive downstream electronics from damaging let-through energy during a short circuit.
  3. Mounting Flexibility: The series is available in both bolt-down and PCB-mount versions, giving designers significant flexibility in how they integrate it into their PDU or converter designs.

Versus the Competition

Compared to similar fuses from other major brands, the Sinofuse BS Series offers a highly competitive performance-to-cost ratio. It meets the key technical specifications required by automotive and industrial customers, such as high breaking capacity and low power dissipation, but at a price point that is very attractive for high-volume production.

Our Verdict

The Sinofuse BS Series is a strong new contender in the auxiliary circuit protection market. Its combination of a high DC voltage rating, compact size, and robust construction makes it an excellent choice for any designer working on EV, ESS, or high-power industrial applications. We have already started recommending the BS Series to several of our key automotive clients for their next-generation PDU designs.
