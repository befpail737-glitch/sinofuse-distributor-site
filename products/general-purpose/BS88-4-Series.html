Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between gG and aR class fuses?

A gG fuse is a general-purpose fuse that provides both overload and short-circuit protection. An aR fuse is a partial-range fuse that provides extremely fast short-circuit protection for semiconductors but may require separate overload protection.

Can I use this fuse in a DC circuit?

While primarily designed for AC, many aR fuses have a DC voltage rating. Please consult the datasheet for the specific DC voltage and interrupting rating for this model.
