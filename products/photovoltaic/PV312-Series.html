Application Guide

Selecting the right fuse for your photovoltaic system is crucial for ensuring safety, reliability, and compliance with industry standards. This guide provides step-by-step instructions for proper fuse selection and installation for various solar applications.

1

Determine System Parameters

Calculate the maximum circuit current based on your solar panel specifications and system configuration. For PV systems, the maximum circuit current is typically 1.25 times the short-circuit current (Isc) of the PV module.

2

Select Appropriate Voltage Rating

Ensure the fuse voltage rating meets or exceeds the system voltage. The PV312 series is rated for both 1000V DC and 1500V DC systems, making it suitable for a wide range of installations.

3

Choose Current Rating

Select a fuse with a current rating that is higher than the maximum continuous current but low enough to protect the circuit conductors. Refer to the current ratings table for available options.

4

Verify Breaking Capacity

Ensure the fuse can safely interrupt the maximum fault current that could occur in your system. The PV312 series offers 80A breaking capacity at 1000V DC.

5

Consider Environmental Factors

Evaluate environmental conditions such as temperature extremes, humidity, and UV exposure. The PV312 series is designed for -40¬∞C to +85¬∞C operation and excellent temperature cycling resistance.
